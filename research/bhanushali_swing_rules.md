## 1. Mindset (Step 1 — before you trade)

- **Accept that losses are part of the game.** Not a slogan — a reality check. Every loss is *feedback*
  telling you what you did right/wrong. **Be a learner, not a blamer.** Trade to *learn*, not to *win*.
- **Remove emotional attachment to money.** Money is a *by-product*, not the purpose. Attachment makes you
  hold losers, book winners too early, and over-size. **Detach from money, attach to mastery.** Money is
  energy — it flows; chasing it raises stress. Detachment ≠ not wanting money; it means money doesn't decide
  your mood. This is the trader's maturity point.
- **Set realistic expectations.** The world's best compound ~**12–20%/year** (Buffett ~19–20% over 60 yrs;
  Peter Lynch ~29% at peak = legendary). 12–20% consistently puts you in the **top ~5%**. Expecting
  20%/month or 200%/year guarantees frustration. Goal ≠ "make a crore" → goal = **"become a consistent,
  confident, disciplined trader."** Process over pace; direction first, speed follows. Market is a marathon.
  "People don't want to get rich slowly." (Buffett to Bezos.)

## 2. Risk management (Step 2 — "execute like a pro")

- **Never risk more than 1–2% of capital per trade.** 1L account → ₹1,000–2,000 max risk per position.
- **Set the stop-loss IMMEDIATELY after entry** (his rule: within ~5 seconds). No "optional" stops.
- **"Never lose BIG money, and never forget rule #1."** (Buffett's "never lose money," adapted — losses are
  inevitable; *big* losses are not.) Math: 10% risk → 10 losses wipe you; 1–2% risk → 10 losses still leave
  80–90% intact, so a comeback is possible. **Survival is the real success.**
- **Exit a losing position without hesitation** when the chart says so (candle low broken, support broken,
  MA broken, trendline broken). The biggest loss comes from *hoping*, not from price falling.
- **Don't fear the "stop-loss hunt."** FII/DII trade in thousands of crores; they will not move a large-cap
  or Nifty 5–10% to take your 50 shares.
- **No-stop cautionary tales:** Reliance Infra (2008, never recovered), DLF, Suzlon, Idea, Lupin (decade
  sideways), **Yes Bank** (₹350–400 blue-chip → penny stock), Satyam (delisted).

## 3. The strategy on the chart

### Trend filter (the watchlist)
- **44-period Simple Moving Average.** Trend timeframe = **weekly** for the RSI system; the pullback system
  uses the **daily** 44-SMA (weekly optional as you advance).
- **Only trade a RISING 44-MA** (bullish). Falling MA → bearish → avoid (or the sell side, which he doesn't
  focus on). Analogy: a student whose marks rose 55→94 *every year* → bet bullish; one that fell 99→38 → don't.
- **⚠ It must be a *visible, sustained* uptrend — not a marginal up-tick.** (He stresses "clearly rising";
  our tests confirm this matters a lot — see appendix.)
- **Weekend routine:** scan the universe (Nifty 100/200/500 — or a free screener like Chartink), keep **only
  the clearly-rising-MA stocks** in a watchlist (~25–50 names). Then Mon–Fri check just those, ~15 min/day.
  Weekend = Fri 3:30 pm → Mon 9:15 am.

### Entry — two setups
- **RSI setup:** on the **daily** chart, RSI(14). Wait for **RSI to dip below 35**, then an **uptick** back
  up **+ a green candle** → **buy ₹1 above that green candle's high**, **stop ₹1 below its low**.
- **44-SMA pullback setup:** price **pulls back to the 44-SMA (support)**, forms a **green candle** →
  **buy above its high**, **stop below its low**.
- **Target:** **1:2 and 1:3** R:R (2× and 3× the entry-to-stop distance). Entry ₹100 / stop ₹90 → targets
  ₹120 and ₹130.
- **RSI settings:** period 14; bands moved to **75 / 35** (35 instead of the traditional 30, because in a
  bull trend stocks often bounce from 33–35; using 30 misses trades). For buying only, the 35 line is enough.

## 4. Patience & timing (Step 4)

- **Wait for your setup; don't force trades.** Not every ball is hit for six. If the setup isn't there, skip
  the day. **Use limit orders** (buy-above-high is itself a limit/stop order — set it and leave it).
- Missed trades are fine — don't chase. **Patience is the ultimate weapon.**

## 5. Position management (Step 5)

- **Check open positions max twice a day.** Watching more doesn't move the market; it only feeds emotion.
- **Hold 3–10 days for the target.** **Do NOT exit on a single red day / shooting star** — one bearish candle
  doesn't move the economy. **Exit only at target or stop** (unless you have a defined trailing rule).
- **Optional trailing stop** to lock profit as price rises.

## 6. RSI — deeper concepts

- **Origin:** Welles Wilder, 1978 ("New Concepts in Technical Trading Systems"). Measures whether the market
  is *tired* (overbought) or *oversold*. Range 0–100, default period 14.
- **Zones:** 0–30 oversold (bounce *possible* — not guaranteed), 30–70 neutral (wait), 70–100 overbought
  (pullback *possible*).
- **The 50-line = trend context**, not an entry. Consistently **above 50 = bullish regime**, below 50 =
  bearish. **Don't trade every 50-crossover** — use it to read the *mahaul* (environment) of the stock.
- **70/75 overbought — do NOT blind-short.** In a strong uptrend price crosses 70/75 repeatedly and keeps
  rising. Overbought ≠ sell signal.
- **Bullish (basic) divergence:** price makes a **lower low**, RSI makes a **higher low** → internal momentum
  strengthening → buy setup (confirm with a green candle; stop below the lower low; 1:2/1:3).
- **Hidden / reverse (positive) divergence:** in an **uptrend**, price makes a **higher low** but RSI makes a
  **lower low** → the move has "room" to continue → continuation buy.
- Divergence **must be confirmed by price action** (green candle / breakout) — never traded in isolation.
- **Practice:** mark **100 charts per strategy** before risking capital.

## 7. Combining RSI with other tools ("dal-chawal" — RSI alone is raw rice)

- **RSI + Bollinger Bands** (world-famous combo): in a bullish weekly regime, buy when price supports the
  **lower Bollinger band**.
- **RSI + Fibonacci:** in a confirmed bull trend, buy the **50% retracement**.
- **RSI + Volume:** buy the breakout on a **high-volume candle (HVC)**.
- **RSI + price action:** double bottom, trendline breakout, support/resistance — confirm the RSI signal.
- Point: **no single indicator makes money.** RSI + MA + price action + volume + **risk management + position
  sizing** is what works. "Indicators don't make money; position sizing and risk management do."

## 8. Liquidity sweep — "Price moves first, news comes later"

- **Big players know before the news; price moves *ahead* of the news.** Examples he cites: Modi PM 2014
  (market bottomed Aug 2013, +40% *before* the result), Yes Bank 2018 (double top + falling MA + red volume
  *before* the crash), Reliance–Jio–Facebook 2020 (price + volume up before the announcement), Netflix Jan
  2022 (double top, −30% *before* the subscriber news), COVID, 2008. This is the **liquidity sweep**.
- **Early-warning signs of a top/crash (before the news):**
  - **Bearish RSI divergence** (price up, RSI down).
  - **Rising red-volume spikes** vs. the prior rally.
  - **Double-top** pattern.
  - **44-MA turning / starting to fall.**
- **"Don't listen to the news — listen to the market."** Study **pure price + volume**.
- **Entry doesn't make money — EXIT does.** Focus as much on *when to sell* as *what to buy*. *Bhav Bhagwan
  che* (price is supreme).

## 9. Mistakes to avoid (his explicit cautions)

- **Don't blind-buy RSI < 30/35** (in his example, 11 consecutive stop-losses without the higher-timeframe
  trend filter).
- **Don't blind-short RSI > 70/75** (strong trends stay overbought and keep rising).
- **Don't use RSI alone** — always combine with trend + price action + volume.
- **Don't switch timeframes randomly** (weekly → daily → 2-min). "A suicide mission." Pick one (ideally
  daily/weekly) and stay.
- **Don't read an RSI value without context** — *how* did it get there matters.
- **Don't expect RSI to call the exact top/bottom** — indicators lag; "a suicide mission."
- **Stops always happen; there is no guarantee** — every tool has losing trades. Always use a stop.
- **Don't chase every fad** (options → crypto → scalping → forex). Are you here to make money or pass time?
- **Trend is your friend until it bends.** Always trade *with* the higher-timeframe trend.

## 10. The daily 6-point checklist (before every trade)

1. Is the **44-MA rising**?
2. Did **price take support on the 44-MA**?
3. Am I **emotionally calm** (not triggered)?
4. Is this **my own analysis** (not a tip)?
5. **Is my stop-loss set** (and will I actually punch it)?
6. **Can I accept this loss** if it hits?

All six "yes" → take the trade. Any "no" → skip it (close the laptop for the day if needed).

## 11. Small things to keep in mind

- Weekend = **Fri 3:30 pm → Mon 9:15 am**. ~1–2 hrs weekend analysis, ~15 min/day weekdays.
- **Start on daily/weekly.** Intraday only after mastery ("first play domestic cricket").
- **Don't marry a stock** — plan the exit *before* entry (book the return ticket first).
- The same principles work on **any timeframe/market** (weekly, daily, 1-hr, 2-min; India, US, etc.) — lower
  timeframes just add more stop-losses, more trades, more patience/mindset load.
- The tool has no power; **the person using it does** (mindset + risk + practice).

## Appendix — what WE measured testing these on our data (2019–2026, ~700 NSE names)

Kept separate from his teaching. Faithful systematic tests; **measurements, not his claims.** Scripts:
`scripts/diag_rsi_system.py`, `scripts/diag_ma44_pullback.py`, `scripts/diag_rsi_weekly.py`,
`scripts/diag_bhanushali_pullback.py`. Finding: [`research/findings/0020-bhanushali-pullback.md`](findings/0020-bhanushali-pullback.md).

- **The oversold-entry half LOSES here.** RSI<35 cross-up entry (−0.24 to −0.42pp forward vs universe) and
  Bollinger lower-band (−0.42pp) both underperform — buying weakness catches falling knives. His core RSI
  system backtests to Sharpe −0.7 with a tight stop.
- **The continuation/momentum half has a small POSITIVE edge.** Hidden divergence (+0.39pp/20d) and HVC volume
  breakout (+0.56pp/20d) beat the universe — "buy strength continuing," not "buy the dip."
- **His emphasis on a *visible* uptrend is empirically right.** A weak "MA ticked up" filter → ~0 edge; a
  *strong sustained* 44-SMA uptrend filter **doubled** the pullback entry edge (−0.11pp → **+0.48pp**/20d).
- **His risk emphasis + our own findings converge on stops:** the *tight candle-low stop he teaches* actually
  destroys the edge (exits on 2–3% noise; Sharpe −0.18). Replacing it with a **wide ATR(2.5×) stop + letting
  winners run (1:3 / 30–40d)** flips the strong-filter pullback to **Sharpe ~+0.5 to +0.9, CAGR +9 to +19%,
  DD −28 to −38%** (2019–26; holds in 2022–26). This matches our own let-winners-run results (0047/0071).
- **Caveats (not yet certified):** that best result is the *max over several stop/exit configs* (selection
  bias — a Deflated-Sharpe test would deflate it), and its Sharpe sits **inside baseline_v1's (0.667)**, so it
  is very likely the **same momentum edge** (buy strong-uptrend pullbacks, let run) in a different wrapper —
  not new alpha. Open checks before any trust: **return-correlation to baseline_v1** (new vs redundant) and a
  **pre-registered CPCV/DSR** pass.

**Net:** his *principles* — trade a clear uptrend, respect risk, give winners room, exit is where money is
made, price leads news — largely hold up and match our own validated findings. His *specific mechanical
entries* (RSI-oversold, Bollinger-oversold) do not carry an edge here; the *momentum-continuation* pieces do.
