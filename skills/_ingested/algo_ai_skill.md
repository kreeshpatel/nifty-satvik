## Credibility: HIGH (with caveats)

This is the single most directly relevant external source for NiftyQuant. It is a working, versioned (v1.1.14) Indian-market algo-trading skill that bakes in NSE/BSE/MCX mechanics, SEBI rules, realistic cost/tax structures, and a backtest→robustness→paper→live lifecycle that mirrors our own promotion discipline. The Indian-market facts (STT/circuit/DPR/tick/T+1/expiry/auction-risk/LTCG) are concrete, current (FY25-26, "Last Updated March 2026"), and verifiable — these are the crown jewels for our `indian-exec` skill. The methodology references (robustness suite, regime, portfolio, risk) are pedagogically solid and well-aligned with our promotion bar.

**Caveats that lower it from "very high":**
1. Most of the skill is execution-oriented and **intraday/F&O-centric** — TWAP/VWAP/iceberg, gamma, max-pain, stop-hunt windows, naked-options. We are a daily-rebalanced, long-only, 63-day-hold cross-sectional **delivery** strategy on yfinance EOD data with no intraday execution and no F&O. ~40% of the content is structurally inapplicable.
2. Many code snippets use `np.random`/placeholder backtests (robustness-testing.md, regime-detection.md) — they teach the *shape* of the test, not a runnable implementation. Port the method, never the body.
3. The regime-detection HMM and the india-data-edge sentiment signals **directly collide with our validated KILLs** (§11: regime-entry-gate KILLED; FII/DII sleeve DEMOTED to regime-only; delivery-% 0010 INCONCLUSIVE/KILL; sector-overlays KILLED). Treat anything in those two files as a re-test-from-scratch hypothesis under skeptical priors, never as adoptable edge.
4. It is a *code-generation* skill ("write me a strategy"), not a research-validation engine. Its promotion bar ("CAGR>30% → demand robustness", "Sharpe>1.0, DD<20%, >50 trades") is far weaker than ours (post-tax ΔSharpe≥+0.10 + ΔCalmar≥+0.05 + 2022-26 positive + WF fold-pass≥60% + bootstrap CI excludes 0 + turnover≤+30% + mechanism). Don't import its thresholds; import its checklists and run them through our gate.

## One-paragraph summary

A production Indian-market (NSE/BSE/MCX) algo-trading code-generation skill covering the full lifecycle: design → backtest with realistic Indian friction → robustness battery → paper → live, with a strict "every strategy must be safe with real money" stance. Its highest-value contributions to us are (a) a precise, current **Indian-market-mechanics reference** — STT/brokerage/exchange-charge rates by instrument, circuit/DPR bands, tick-size rounding, T+1, NSE-has-no-public-API, short-auction risk, expiry calendar, STCG/LTCG 7.5% arbitrage; (b) a **robustness test suite** (walk-forward, Monte-Carlo trade-reshuffle, Monte-Carlo price-permutation, 30% untouched holdout, regime-conditional, ±10% sensitivity, min-trade-count gate) that maps almost 1:1 onto our Phase-4 harness; (c) **portfolio-construction patterns** (Sharpe-weighted-with-correlation-penalty allocation, true correlation-aware portfolio heat, sector/position caps, Calmar optimization, crisis-correlation stress test) that feed our conviction-sizing and risk overlays; and (d) **layered psychological/drawdown guardrails** (daily/weekly/monthly circuit breakers, consecutive-loss pause, decay-detection via rolling Sharpe) for our operating model. We reject its intraday-execution, F&O/options, and sentiment-signal material as out-of-scope or KILL-colliding, and we never import its weak promotion thresholds.

### Distilled portable content (ready to adapt)

**P1 — Cost-model reconciliation checklist (indian-exec / data-quality).** Audit `engine/backtest_engine` + `config.py` against this rate card: STT delivery (reconcile our sell-side-0.1% vs source's "both legs" claim — the source's own §1 table says delivery STT is buy-side 0.1% "Paid on both buy and sell", which conflicts with the simple sell-side model; resolve against the actual Zerodha/SEBI schedule, this is the load-bearing number for post-tax ΔSharpe), brokerage 0.03-0.05%/leg, exchange txn charge ~0.003%, SEBI turnover ~0.0001%, stamp duty (buy-side, small), slippage tier by ADV (0.05% liquid / 0.1-0.2% illiquid — we already have capacity-aware sqrt-impact + 5% ADV cap, so cross-check the constant). Output: a one-line confirmation per cost component that the backtester charges it, or a logged omission.

**P2 — Tick + DPR emission guard (indian-exec).** Before any signal writes an entry/stop/target: `round_to_tick(price, tick)` and assert stop/target within prev_close±circuit-band. For the EOD long-horizon cron this is mostly a *live-execution* concern (Kite) and a penny-stock stop-distortion guard (our known F2). Port as a small util + an assertion, not a new dependency.

**P3 — Two harness gates worth adding (harness / research-discipline).** (a) *Trade-order Monte-Carlo reshuffle*: shuffle the realized trade sequence N=1000×, recompute terminal P&L; if the real sequence sits >75th pctile it's a lucky ordering — a cheap luck check for any overlay before the heavier bootstrap. (b) *±10% parameter sensitivity map*: perturb each FROZEN knob ±10%, confirm Sharpe/Calmar move smoothly (plateau, not spike). Both are method-only; implement against our real engine, discard the source's `np.random` placeholder bodies. Keep our matched-permutation IC null (AUD-022) over their price-permutation.

**P4 — Calmar helper (harness).** `calmar = (cum[-1]**(252/n) − 1) / |min(drawdown)|`. Drop into the scorecard so ΔCalmar≥+0.05 is computed identically everywhere.

**P5 — Rolling-Sharpe decay monitor (indian-exec / harness).** `rolling_sharpe = mean/std × √252` over a window; flag when < floor. Generalize our existing 30d-Sharpe rollback trigger into a continuous paper/live monitor. **Re-derive the window + floor for a 63-day-hold strategy** (a 30-day window is too short for a 3-month-hold book).

**P6 — Correlation-aware portfolio heat (conviction).** When Phase-6 sizing or a 2nd sleeve lands: size on `√(wᵀ Cov w)`, not additive notional; compute marginal risk contribution per name. Gate any heat-aware weighting against the "weighting inert under caps" (0020) prior — prove it beats the flat cap before adopting.

**P7 — Strategy-lint CI (research-discipline).** Adapt `validate_strategy.py`'s AST rules to our cron: FAIL on hardcoded Kite tokens / direct NSE scraping; WARN on missing IST tz / missing structured logging. Cheap, runs in existing CI.
